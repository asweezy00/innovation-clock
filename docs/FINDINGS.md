## 4. Rare-disease (orphan) cut

_Source: real FDA Orphan Drug Designations (OOPD) database (`src/fetch_orphan.py`), not a heuristic._

- Negotiated drugs with ≥1 FDA-approved orphan indication: **19**.
- Carrying ≥2 distinct approved orphan indications (multi-orphan): **12** (Austedo, Calquence, Enbrel, Entyvio, Imbruvica, Lenvima, Ofev, Orencia, Otezla, Pomalyst, Stelara, Xifaxan).
- **Strict serial-orphan** (OBBBA test: multiple orphan indications AND *no* approved non-orphan indication) = **4**: Calquence, Imbruvica, Ofev, Pomalyst.

Orphan/non-orphan basis for each multi-orphan candidate:

- **Imbruvica** — CLL/SLL, Waldenström's macroglobulinemia, chronic graft-versus-host disease — all rare/orphan; no non-orphan approval. → ✓ serial-orphan
- **Calquence** — Mantle cell lymphoma, CLL/SLL — all rare/orphan; no non-orphan approval. → ✓ serial-orphan
- **Ofev** — Idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis, chronic fibrosing ILD, SSc-ILD — all rare/orphan (US label); no non-orphan approval. → ✓ serial-orphan
- **Pomalyst** — Multiple myeloma, Kaposi sarcoma — both orphan; no non-orphan approval. → ✓ serial-orphan
- **Lenvima** — Differentiated thyroid cancer (orphan) BUT also renal cell carcinoma and endometrial carcinoma — NON-orphan -> disqualified. → ✗ NOT serial-orphan

## 5. Balance check — what an EPIC-style 11-year rule would do

EPIC moves **only the small-molecule** selection clock (yr 7 → 11); biologics are already at yr 11 and are **unaffected**, so they cannot be removed by EPIC. Of the **30 negotiated small molecules**, **19** were less than 11 years past first approval at their selection date — i.e. they would have been **unreachable** under an 11-year rule — representing **$67.6B** of 2023 Medicare spend.

- Cycle 3: 6 of 8 small molecules blocked.
